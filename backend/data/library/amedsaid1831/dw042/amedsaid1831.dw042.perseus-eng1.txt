1.1 Omar to Sheikh Hunter:
1.2 You asked me to write my life, but I am not able to write my life, for I have forgotten much of my language along with the Arabic language.
1.3 I cannot read grammatically except a little, or linguistically except a little.
1.4 Oh my brother, I ask you by God not to blame me, for my eyes are weak and my body is likewise.
1.5 My name is Omar Ibn Said of the birthplace of Fuut of Turu between the two seas.
1.6 I sought educational provisions under a sheik named Mohammed Said and my brothers Suleiman Kembeh and Sheik Jabril Abdel.
1.7 I remained a student of learning for 25 years.
1.8 I returned to my place for six years. 
1.9 There came to our country a great army.
2.1 It killed many people, made me go to the greatest sea, sold me into the hand of the Christian who bought me and moved me to a large ship of the great sea.
2.2 It moved on the great sea for a month and half a month and came to a place called Charleston in the words of the Christian and they sold me. 
2.3 A small, weak little man of evil bought me.
2.4 He was called Johnson.
2.5 A great infidel, he did not fear God at all.
2.6 I am a small man not able to work intensely.
2.7 I fled from the hand of Johnson.
2.8 For months I walked to a place called Fayetteville.
2.9 I saw houses.
2.10 For months I entered into the houses to pray.
2.11 I saw a boy riding a horse.
2.12 The boy came to the place of his father and informed his father that he saw me, a Black man, in the prominent house.
2.13 A man named Hunter and one other man among them riding horses with many dogs took me to go with them twelve miles to a place called Fayetteville, to a big house that I was not able to leave.
2.14 I remained in the big house called jail in the Christian language for 16 days and nights.
2.15 On Friday I went to the big one who opened the door of the house.
2.16 I saw lots of men all of them Christian.
2.17 He called to me, “Is your name Omar or Said?”
2.18 I couldn’t hear the Christian words.
2.19 I saw a man called Bob Mumford who concealed me and sent me out of the big house.
2.20 I rejoiced greatly.
2.21 I went with him to his place.
2.22 I remained at the Mumford place four nights and days.
2.23 A man named Jim Owen, the husband of Mumford’s daughter, Betsy Mumford, asked me if I was willing to go to the place called Bladen.
2.24 Yes, I was willing to go with them.
2.25 I remained at the place of Jim Owen until now.
3.1 Before bringing me to the hand of General Owen, a man named Mitchell bought me. 
3.2 Mitchell came to me and asked me to come to the place of Charleston.
3.3 I said no, no, no, no, no, no, no I wouldn’t go to the place of Charleston.
3.4 I remained in the hand of Jim Owen
3.5 Oh people of North Carolina, oh people of South Carolina, oh people of America, all of them, are there among them two righteous men like these righteous men called Jim Owen and John Owen?
3.6 All of the food that I am fed, all of the clothes I wear, they give me.
3.7 Jim and his brother read the gospel of God our lord, our savior, and our king and reformer of our state of being, our condition and our wealth, generously, not requisitely, according to his power.
3.8 I open my heart to the right way, to the way of Jesus the Messiah to a great light.
4.1 Before coming to the Christian land my religion was the religion of Muhammad, the prophet of God, God bless him and grant him peace.
4.2 I went to the mosque before the rising of dawn, washed my face and head and hands and feet.
4.3 I prayed at noon time.
4.4 I prayed in the time after.  
4.5 I prayed at the time of sunset.
4.6 I prayed at evening time.
4.7 I prayed.
4.8 I gave zakat every year, gold and silver and seed and cows and sheep and goats or rice and wheat and barley.
4.9 I gave all this as zakat, sending it for jihad every year against the infidels.
4.10 I went to Mecca and Medina with whoever was able.
5.1 My father has six sons and five daughters.
5.2 My mother has three sons and one daughter.
5.3 As for me, on the day I left our country my age was 37 years.
5.4 I have remained in the Christian country 24 years.
6.1 In the year 1831 of Jesus the Messiah.
7.1 Oh people of North Carolina, oh people of South Carolina, oh American people, all of them!
7.2 The first son of Jim Owen is called Thomas, and his other daughter is called Martha Jane. 
7.3 This family is a good family. 
7.4 Tom Owen and Nell Owen have two sons and one daughter
7.5 The first son is call Jim and the other John and the daughter is called Melissa.
7.6 Mr. Jim and Betsy Owen, his wife, have two sons and five daughters.
7.7 The first is Tom, John, Mercy, Sophia, Margaret, and Eliza.
7.8 This family is a very good family.
7.9 And John Owen and his wife called Luisa, a righteous wife, had three and two children. 
7.10 Three died and two remain.
8.1 Oh people of America, oh people of North Carolina, is there among you? Is there among you? Is there among you? Is there among you as good a family, fearing God greatly?
8.2 I, Omar, love to read the book of the great Qur’an
8.3 General Jim Owen and his wife read the gospel.
8.4 They read a lot to me the gospel of God, our lord, our creator, our king, and reformer of our present state of being, condition and wealth, generously, not requisitely, according to his power.
8.5 Open my heart to the gospel, to the right way.
8.6 Thanks be to God, lord of all worlds, many thanks.
8.7 He is bountiful in blessing who increases in goodness.
9.1 So that the law was given by Moses and grace and truth were by Jesus Christ.
10.1 To pray, Muhammad said first: praise to God, Lord of Worlds, the Merciful, the Compassionate, Possessor of the Day of Judgment, you we worship, from you we seek aid.
10.2 Guide us on the straight path, the path of those on whom you bestow grace, not those against whom you are angered, and not those who are astray.
10.3 Amen.
11.1 And now he prays the words of our Lord Jesus Christ:
11.2 Oh our Father, who is in the heavens, your name is holy.
11.3 May your kingdom come, your will come to be, as in heaven and upon earth.
11.4 Give us our bread which is for tomorrow, today, and forgive us what is against us, like we forgive him against whom we have something.
11.5 And do not lead us to temptation, but bring us from evil.
11.6 For yours is the kingdom and the power and the glory forever.
11.7 Amen.
11.8 I stay in our land because of great harm.
11.9 Infidels did a great injustice and sold me into the hand of Christians.
11.10 I was purchased and went on the great sea for a month and half-a-month to a place called Charleston in the Christian speech.
11.11 I was driven into the hand of a small, weak and evil little man, who did not fear God at all, who did not read nor pray.
11.12 I was afraid to stay with this evil man, great in his sins.
11.13 I fled for months.
11.14 God our Lord brought us forward to the hand of a pious man who feared God and loved to do righteous deeds.
11.15 He was called General Jim Owen
11.16 With their brother called Colonel John Owen.
11.17 These are two pious men.
11.18 I remain in the place Bladen County.
12.1 I remain in the hand of Jim Owen.
12.2 He does not beat me.
12.3 He does not keep me nailed down.
12.4 I do not hunger.
12.5 I am not naked.
12.6 I do not labor intensely for I am not able to labor intense labor for I am a small, ill man.
12.7 I have remained twenty years, not seeing anything harmful from the hand of Jim Owen.
